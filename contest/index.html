CenturyLink Panamax App Template Challenge 2014

Submit your creative, popular or innovative Panamax App Template. Over $100,000+ in prizes total are up for grabs. 60 finalists from multiple categories will receive a Mac Pro or iPad Air.

Over $100,000 in Prizes

– 30 Mac Pros, a $3,999.00 value * 3.5GHz 6-core with 12MB of L3 cache * 16GB (4x4GB) of 1866MHz DDR3 ECC * 256GB PCIe-based flash storage * Dual AMD FirePro D500 GPUs with 3GB of GDDR5 VRAM each – 30 iPad Airs, a $499.00 value * 16GB * Wi-Fi Only – Copies of the Docker Book by James Turnbull – Winners will be featured on the CenturyLink Labs Blog and Newsletter – Fun SWAG: Stickers, T-shirts and iPhone Dots

Contest Structure

The contest officially begins on August 12, 2014 at 12:01a Pacific. Part 1) The first 2 weeks are for submitting the best and most creative templates you can think of (submission process listed below). All contest submissions must be submitted by August 26, 2014 at 12:01a Pacific. There will then be a one week quiet period where the submissions are validated and bundled into the mainline Panamax distribution. Part 2) There will then be 1 week to promote your templates to friends and colleagues starting on September 2nd, 2014 at 12:01a Pacific and will last 1 week ending at September 9, 2014 at 12:01a Pacific. All submitted templates that pass our validation will be distributed to anyone downloading Panamax during this time. Part 3) Judging will be a 2 week process starting September 9th, 2014 and will be finished by September 23rd, 2014. Winners will be announced by September 23rd, 2014

How to Submit a Contest Entry

Step 1) Install Panamax using the directions from http://panamax.io/get-panamax.html Step 2) Create the best template you can think of, using the guidelines listed in the documentation at http://panamax.io/documentation Step 3) Fork the official the official Panamax Template Repository from https://github.com/CenturyLinkLabs/panamax-contest-templates to your personal GitHub account Step 4) Press "Save as Template" for your new application and save your template to your forked panamax-contest-templates repository Step 5) Once your template is ready, go to GitHub and submit a pull request from your forked panamax-contest-templates repository, make sure to include any additional information in the comments of the pull request like how you are promoting it (urls to blog posts/twitter mentions/etc.).

Judging Criteria

Half of the prizes will go to the most popular template authors and the other half of the prizes will go to a list of subjective criteria that will be judged by a panel of independent judges who are also members of the Docker ecosystem. 15 Mac Pros will go to 15 Template Authors with the Top 15 Most Popular Templates measured by a combination of community use & engagement with your template, and social media mentions of your contest entry. 15 iPad Airs will go to 15 Runner-Up Template Authors with the Next Top 15 Most Popular Templates measured by a combination of community use & engagement with your template, and social media mentions of your contest entry. 5 Mac Pros will go to 5 Template Authors that the Judging Panel deems as Judge's Choice 5 iPad Airs will go to 5 Template Authors that the Judging Panel deems as Runner Up Judge's Choice 5 Mac Pro will go to 5 Template Authors that the Judging Panel deems a Template with the Best Accompanying Blog Post 5 iPad Air will go to 5 Template Authors that the Judging Panel deems a Runner Up Template with the Best Accompanying Blog Post 3 Mac Pros will go to 3 Template Authors with the Most GitHub Stars on their Templates Repository 3 iPad Airs will go to 3 Template Authors with the Runner Up Most GitHub Stars on their Templates Repository 1 Mac Pro will go to 1 Person that the Judging Panel deems has given the Best Feedback/Suggestion for Panamax Improvement (with input based on UserVoice survey) 1 iPad Air will go to 1 Person that the Judging Panel deems has given the Runner Up Best Feedback/Suggestion for Panamax Improvement (with input based on UserVoice survey) 1 Mac Pro will go to the 1 Person that the Judging Panel deems as having contributed the Most Documentation Improvements 1 iPad Air will go to the 1 Person that the Judging Panel deems as having contributed the Runner Up Most Documentation Improvements

Rules

Only one prize can be won per person (if you submit multiple pull requests with multiple app templates, you can only win for one of the app templates) Any individual person can only win one prize in the entire contest (you can't win multiple prizes in multiple categories) Images used in templates must be available publicly on the Docker hub (https://registry.hub.docker.com/). Cannot use local or images in private repositories. All templates must include documentation in the documentation section including the following: System requirements (for example: for best performance, you need 2 cores and 2GB of RAM) Application setup (for example: what environmental variables or ports need to be set) Post-Run instructions (For example: what username and password do I need to access my application) Information regarding port-forwarding back to the host machine to hit a url Criteria that the app is running up and running correctly No dependence on outside resources apart from code and containers No templates that create apps intended for malicious or illegal purposes
